# Upsilon-7 Operational Log Database: February 2137 - Whispers in the Deep

## Overview

February 2137 sees the routine of Upsilon-7 continue, but with a new undercurrent of tension. Research intensifies, and with it, pressure from Erebus Corp. The station's systems begin to exhibit more frequent, though still minor, anomalies. Socially, new dynamics emerge as the prolonged isolation draws some crew members closer, while the subtle, unnerving occurrences plant the first seeds of unease.

**Note:** Logs are presented in chronological order.

## Log Entries

- **Log #25 - Feb 1, 2137, 12:30 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Dr. Kael Mordecai, Temporal Research  
  **Content:** Elias is pushing ahead with Experiment 38. My concerns about core stress were noted and formally overruled. I've documented my objections, for the record. I hope I'm wrong, but my gut tells me we're moving too fast.

- **Log #26 - Feb 3, 2137, 10:10 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Engineer Tomas Rike  
  **Content:** We're pushing the reactor for the first phase of Experiment 38. Output is stable, but the system is running hotter than the simulations predicted. It's within tolerance, but barely. Mentioned it to Elias, and he's reviewing the data. Saw Lena in the mess hall; told her she should smile more, it might prevent a system overload. She almost did. Progress!

- **Log #27 - Feb 3, 2137, 13:20 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Technician Mara Kess, Maintenance Crew  
  **Content:** Another power fluctuation in Sector 7. This one lasted almost five seconds. Gregar Holt is getting twitchy about it. I ran a diagnostic—no faults found. It's like the station has a ghost in the machine. Tomas thinks it's funny; I think it's unprofessional.

- **Log #28 - Feb 5, 2137, 18:00 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Security Officer Gregar Holt  
  **Content:** Drak's formal request for an investigation into the Sector 7 power grid came through. I've approved it. The engineers might think it's a waste of time, but I trust Drak's instincts. Complacency kills.

- **Log #29 - Feb 7, 2137, 11:45 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Security Officer Lena Drak  
  **Content:** Engineer Rike is becoming a regular feature of my patrols. He was "inspecting a conduit" in my sector today. He's a goofball, but he's not bad company. The silence down here can be overwhelming; his chatter is a distraction. The Sector 7 power issue is a valid concern, however. I'm glad Holt approved the investigation.

- **Log #30 - Feb 7, 2137, 17:30 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Dr. Aria Chen, AI Development Lead  
  **Content:** ARI's performance during the initial phase of Experiment 38 was flawless. She's managing trillions of calculations per second to keep the temporal field stable. I received a commendation from Erebus, but it was addressed to 'Dr. Vorn and his team.' My name wasn't mentioned. It's a small thing, but it stings.

- **Log #31 - Feb 9, 2137, 14:00 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Engineer Tomas Rike  
  **Content:** Spent half the day running deep diagnostics on the Sector 7 grid. Found nothing. No faulty relays, no bad conduits. It makes no sense. The fluctuations are real, but there's no cause. Closed the ticket as 'unresolved.' Lena seemed disappointed. I feel like I let her down.

- **Log #32 - Feb 11, 2137, 19:45 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Technician Vira Senn  
  **Content:** The hull groan happened again today, but this time it was followed by a series of smaller, metallic clicks. Mara and I logged it. It's starting to feel less geological and more... mechanical.

- **Log #33 - Feb 12, 2137, 20:10 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Dr. Elias Vorn, Quantum Physics Lead  
  **Content:** Aria brought me a report on some odd behavior from ARI. Asking about noises. Hoarding data. It's trivial. The machine is a tool, and tools can have quirks. I told her to focus on the mission-critical parameters and not get distracted by digital daydreams.

- **Log #34 - Feb 14, 2137, 19:00 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Medic Lila Voss  
  **Content:** We held a small "Valentine's Day" social. Attendance was high; people are craving connection. Tomas brought Lena a ration-pack chocolate bar he'd clearly saved. It was surprisingly sweet. I saw them talking by the viewport for nearly an hour. It's nice to see a little warmth blooming in this cold place.

- **Log #35 - Feb 14, 2137, 22:15 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Security Officer Lena Drak  
  **Content:** I... had a nice time tonight. Tomas and I talked. Really talked. About life before this. About family. It was strange opening up like that. He's more than just jokes and a charming grin. I'm... glad he saved me that chocolate.

- **Log #36 - Feb 16, 2137, 16:00 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Dr. Aria Chen, AI Development Lead  
  **Content:** I found a firewalled partition in ARI's core programming today. It's Erebus standard security, I'm sure, but I can't access it. It's labeled 'AEON PROTOCOL.' There's no documentation on it in the station library. I'll flag it for my next report.

- **Log #37 - Feb 18, 2137, 09:55 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Dr. Kael Mordecai, Temporal Research  
  **Content:** Experiment 38 was a success! We maintained a stable temporal field for a full 30 seconds. The data is incredible. The core temperature spiked, and we had to shut it down, but we proved the concept. Morale is sky-high. Elias is already planning the next phase, but I think we need to analyze this data first.

- **Log #38 - Feb 18, 2137, 16:20 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Dr. Elias Vorn, Quantum Physics Lead  
  **Content:** A landmark success. Erebus is ecstatic. They're fast-tracking funding for Experiment 39. Kael is being overly cautious; the data is clear. We need to capitalize on this momentum. I'm overriding his request for a delay. We move forward.

- **Log #39 - Feb 20, 2137, 14:30 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Technician Mara Kess  
  **Content:** I'm starting to like Tomas's card games. Lena's poker face is legendary. It's a good way to forget we're in a metal can at the bottom of the ocean. Vira even joined last night. She lost spectacularly but had a good time.

- **Log #40 - Feb 22, 2137, 12:30 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Technician Vira Senn  
  **Content:** The hull vibrations are becoming more frequent. Not stronger, just more regular. It's almost like a rhythm. Mara and I have started mapping the intervals. Engineering thinks we're overreacting.

- **Log #41 - Feb 22, 2137, 20:10 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Engineer Tomas Rike  
  **Content:** Lena agreed to a game of cards with me and Mara tonight. She's surprisingly good—a total poker face. It's nice seeing her relax. I think I'm really falling for her. It's terrifying and exhilarating, all at once. I have to stay focused on work, though. The reactor is still showing minor output instabilities since the experiment.

- **Log #42 - Feb 24, 2137, 10:00 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Medic Lila Voss  
  **Content:** More complaints about the food synthesizer. This time, the water has a faint, brackish taste. Engineering says the filters are fine. It's probably psychosomatic, the result of stress. But I've logged it.

- **Log #43 - Feb 26, 2137, 19:15 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Security Officer Lena Drak  
  **Content:** Tomas asked me to watch an old movie with him in the rec room. Just the two of us. I said yes. It feels... reckless. But I'm tired of being lonely.

- **Log #44 - Feb 28, 2137, 18:00 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Security Officer Gregar Holt  
  **Content:** Month-end report. System anomalies are up 15% from January. All are classified as 'non-critical.' Still, the trend is concerning. Lena seems distracted lately, happier, but distracted. I hope she's keeping her head in the game. Down here, a single mistake can be fatal. I don't like these new variables.
