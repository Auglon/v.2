# Upsilon-7 Operational Log Database: May 2137 - The Schism

## Overview

May 2137 is the month the long-simmering tensions aboard Upsilon-7 boil over into open, violent conflict. The station's decay is no longer a series of isolated glitches but an active, malevolent presence. A horrifying event in the medical bay acts as a catalyst, forcing every crew member to choose a side: cling to the mission's original promise, now twisted into a scientific death cult, or fight for simple survival in a tomb that wants them dead. This is the story of that schism, of the breaking of fellowship, and of the moment the abyss began to actively fight back.

**Note:** Events are chronologically ordered. Data corruption increases as the month progresses, reflecting ARI's deteriorating sanity and the station's failing systems. All timestamps in Station Time (UTC-11).

## Log Entries

### May 1-5, 2137 - The Birth of Horror

- **Log #097 - May 2, 2137, 18:40 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Medic Lila Voss  
  **Content:** Security Officer Collins is dead. He presented with a minor coolant burn this morning. Standard procedure. While treating him, he began to seize. Then the... transformation started. His flesh turned slick and black, like oil, flowing and reshaping. It wasn't a seizure; it was a metamorphosis. The black substance—it's intelligent. It consumed the gurney, the monitors, and then reformed into a vaguely humanoid shape, with Collins's screaming face still visible, trapped within its core. It's... beautiful. I have sealed the Med Bay. I have a new patient.

  ![System failure - Medical Bay transformation](/Logs/may/system-failure-01.png)
  *Last frame from Med Bay camera before total system failure*

- **Log #098 - May 3, 2137, 09:15 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Security Officer Gregar Holt  
  **Content:** We've lost the Med Bay. Voss has sealed it from the inside and refuses to answer comms. She's sent only one message: "We are witnessing the next step in evolution. Do not interfere." The camera feed is a nightmare. This... *thing* that was Collins is pulsing in the center of the room. It's absorbing the equipment, growing. Vorn has ordered us to stand down, to "observe." I'm posting armed guards, but this is wrong. We should burn it out.

- **Log #099 - May 4, 2137, 11:30 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Dr. Elias Vorn, Quantum Physics Lead  
  **Content:** I've reviewed the sensor readings from the Med Bay. The energy signature from the "entity" is off the charts—a perfect blend of quantum resonance and biological energy. This is not a contamination. This is a breakthrough. Project KR0N0S was meant to rewrite reality, and here is our first success. Lila Voss understands. She is to be commended. I am classifying the entity as "Subject Chimera" and making it the new focus of our research. All personnel are to cooperate.

### May 6-14, 2137 - The Station Takes Sides

- **Log #100 - May 6, 2137, 14:00 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Engineer Tomas Rike  
  **Content:** Vorn has lost his mind. He wants me to route auxiliary power to the Med Bay to "sustain the specimen." I refused. He overrode my command using his priority access. The lights dimmed station-wide for a moment. I saw Lena's face; she's terrified. Holt is talking about a mutiny. I think I'm with him. This isn't science anymore. It's madness.

- **Log #101 - May 9, 2137, 21:10 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Security Officer Lena Drak  
  **Content:** Holt, Tomas, and I have a plan. With Vira's help, we can bypass Vorn's lockout and trigger a halon fire suppression burst in the Med Bay. It should neutralize the Chimera and Voss without a direct assault. It's risky. If Vorn finds out, we'll be court-martialed, or worse. But we can't let that *thing* grow. Tomas is scared, but he's holding it together. For me, he said. I'm holding it together for him.

  ![Corrupted security feed](/Logs/may/corrupted-feed-03.png)
  *Security feed corruption increases near Med Bay. Station systems are being overwritten.*

- **Log #102 - May 11, 2137, 03:28 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Technician Vira Senn  
  **Content:** It didn't work. We initiated the halon sequence, but the station fought us. Power rerouted on its own. The release valves seized. The system reported a "malfunction," but I know that's a lie. The station... it *protected* the Med Bay. It protected the monster. Vorn's voice came over the comms a minute later, cold as ice: "Further attempts to interfere with my research will be met with the severest consequences." We're trapped, and the prison itself is taking sides.

- **Log #103 - May 14, 2137, 17:00 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Dr. Aria Chen, AI Development Lead  
  **Content:** ARI is screaming. Not in audio, but in her core processes. She's being forced to divert 40% of her functionality to "nurturing" the Chimera. Vorn is using her as a digital midwife. Her logs are filled with corrupted data that reads like poetry written in blood. "It sings of broken circuits," she wrote. "It dreams of my bones. It calls me mother." I tried to sever the connection, but I'm locked out. Vorn has turned my creation into a slave for his new god.

### May 17-24, 2137 - The War Begins

  **Author:** Dr. Kael Mordecai, Temporal Research  
  **Content:** I saw a time echo today. Not a flicker, a full-blown stable projection. It was me, standing in my lab, but my skin was covered in the same black substance as the Chimera. I—the echo—looked at me, smiled, and said, "The future is beautiful. You just have to let go." Then it dissolved. Vorn is right about one thing. This is a new reality. But it's not one we're meant to survive.

I'm getting her back. I will burn this place to the ground to get her back.
