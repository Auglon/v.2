# Upsilon-7 Operational Log Database: March 2137 - Love in the Deep

## Overview

March 2137: The month where human connection bloomed in the crushing depths. As the station's systems showed the first signs of strain, two souls found solace in each other. This visual record captures not just the technical logs, but the moments between - stolen glances, shared laughter, and the desperate hope that love could survive even here, 11,000 meters below the surface.

**Note:** Enhanced with recovered visual data from security feeds and personal devices.

## Log Entries

### March 9, 2137 - The Card Game

- **Log #011 - March 9, 2137, 13:10 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Technician Mara Kess, Maintenance Crew  
  **Content:** Repaired the lighting in Sector 7. Just a loose connection, nothing major, but it took longer than expected. Tomas swung by to "supervise," mostly just to chat. He's relentless with the card game invites. I finally agreed, mostly to shut him up. Lena's joining too—should be fun, though I'm terrible at cards. Noticed a faint vibration in the hull while working; probably just pressure shifts, but it's logged.

- **Log #012 - March 9, 2137, 21:35 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Security Officer Lena Drak  
  **Content:** Card game was a blast. Tomas is a terrible bluffer, but he's got everyone laughing. Mara's surprisingly competitive—kept catching me off guard with her plays. We stayed up later than planned, swapping stories about life before Upsilon-7. Tomas walked me back to quarters after; he's sweet when he's not teasing. Felt a bit of that old butterflies-in-the-stomach thing. Nice to feel human for a night.

### March 11, 2137 - The Lingering Goodbye

- **Log #013 - March 11, 2137, 10:20 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Engineer Tomas Rike  
  **Content:** Power grid lag Vira noted is resolved—just a calibration issue. Systems back to normal. More importantly, card game night was a success. Mara's got a sharp wit when she opens up, and Lena... well, let's just say I'm looking forward to the next game. I might've lingered a bit too long saying goodnight to her. Also, noticed a slight uptick in reactor output fluctuations today—within norms, but I'll keep an eye on it.

### March 17, 2137 - By the Viewport

- **Log #020 - March 17, 2137, 15:25 (Station Time)**  
  **Author:** Security Officer Lena Drak  
  **Content:** Patrols uneventful. Helped Tomas move some equipment in Sector 6 after my shift—more an excuse to talk than anything. He's easy to be around, makes the endless metal walls feel less suffocating. We lingered by the viewport, watching the dark ocean. He brushed my hand; I didn't pull away. Not sure what this is, but it's... nice.

## Epilogue

These images were recovered from various devices after the events of April-July 2137. They serve as a reminder that even in the darkest depths, human connection persists. That love, however brief, burned bright against the crushing dark of the deep ocean.

The quantum core's stability readings for March averaged 87.3%. By April, they would drop below 60%.

But in March, there was still hope. There was still love.

There was still time.
